## 🧠 The One-Sentence Pitch
Writing is not the outcome of thinking; writing is the medium in which thinking takes place, and a bottom-up note-taking system (Zettelkasten) allows insights to emerge naturally over time.

## 🔑 Key Takeaways & Highlights

### ⚡ Fleeting Notes vs. Permanent Notes
- **Fleeting notes** are just quick reminders of thoughts. They are temporary and must be processed within a day or two. (In this vault, these go into `00 Inbox`).
- **Literature notes** summarize books/articles you read in your own words.
- **Permanent notes** are self-contained ideas (Evergreen Notes) that are fully written out, ready to be published or used. They are stored in `20 Brain` and linked to other notes.

### 🕸️ Bottom-Up Thinking
- Do not start with a rigid hierarchy or pre-conceived table of contents.
- Let categories and topics emerge from the bottom up by connecting individual ideas.
- As you link note A to note B, clusters of thoughts naturally form. This completely eliminates writer's block.
